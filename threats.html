Common Online Threats

Knowing the dangers is the first step to staying safe online.

Phishing

Fake messages or emails that pretend to be from trusted sources to trick you into giving passwords, money, or personal information.

ℹAlways verify the sender before clicking any link.

Malware

Harmful software like viruses, spyware, or ransomware that can damage your device or steal your data.

How to avoid: Install updates and avoid downloading unknown files.

Identity Theft

When someone steals your personal information and pretends to be you — often to commit fraud or scams.

How to avoid: Use strong passwords and keep personal info private.

Cyberbullying

Harassment or hurtful behavior online through messages, posts, or threats — common on social media.

How to avoid: Block/report bullies and save evidence.

Weak Password Attacks

Hackers can guess or break simple passwords using automated tools.

How to avoid: Use strong, unique, mixed-character passwords.

Data Breaches

When hackers break into websites or apps and leak users’ personal information.

How to avoid: Don’t reuse passwords across websites.

Fake Websites

Scam websites designed to look real but steal money or info when you enter details.

How to avoid: Check URLs carefully before entering info.

Bots & Scams

Fake bot accounts on social media or messaging apps that trick you into giving information or clicking harmful links.

How to avoid: Avoid unknown profiles or too-good-to-be-true offers.

Deepfakes

AI-generated fake videos or images that look real and are used to spread lies, scams, or embarrassment.

How to avoid: Verify information before believing or sharing.
