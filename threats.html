Common Online Threats

Understand the risks you face online—and how to avoid them.

Phishing Attacks

Fraudulent emails, messages, or websites designed to trick users into revealing sensitive information such as passwords or banking details.

Examples: Fake account alerts, suspicious links from unknown senders.

ℹ Verify the sender before clicking any link and avoid sharing personal information online.

Malware (Malicious Software)

Software specifically designed to infiltrate, damage, or gain unauthorized access to devices.

Examples: Viruses, trojans, spyware, and worms.

ℹ Install reputable antivirus software, update your system regularly, and avoid unverified downloads.

Identity Theft

Unauthorized use of personal data to impersonate someone online, often leading to financial loss or reputational damage.

Examples: Accounts accessed without permission, fake profiles created in your name.

ℹ Use strong, unique passwords, enable two-factor authentication, and never share sensitive information.

Cyberbullying

Intentional harassment, intimidation, or humiliation conducted through digital platforms.

Examples: Offensive comments, spreading false information, impersonation.

ℹ Block and report offenders, maintain strict privacy settings, and seek guidance from trusted adults.

Weak Password Exploitation

Attempts to compromise accounts by guessing commonly used or simple passwords.

Examples: Breaches caused by “123456”, “password”, or reused passwords.

ℹ Create long, complex passwords and enable multi-factor authentication.

Data Breaches

Security incidents where confidential user information is exposed due to hacking or vulnerabilities.

Examples: Leaked credentials, unauthorized access to personal databases.

ℹ Update passwords regularly and avoid reusing them across platforms.

Fake or Fraudulent Websites

Websites made to impersonate legitimate ones and steal personal or financial details.

Examples: Misspelled URLs, websites asking for unusual information.

ℹ Verify URLs, look for HTTPS, and only transact on trusted websites.

Bots & Online Scams

Automated programs and deceptive schemes made to manipulate users into sharing information or clicking harmful links.

Examples: Spam DMs, fake prize notifications, bot-generated chats.

ℹ Avoid unknown messages and utilize strong spam filters.

Deepfakes

Artificially manipulated audio, video, or images created to misrepresent individuals or events.

Examples: Synthetic videos of public figures, fake statements attributed to someone.

ℹ Verify sources before believing or sharing digital content.
