Need Help? Here’s What You Can Do

Official reporting portals, hotlines, and steps to stay safe if something goes wrong online.

Report Cybercrime Online

Use the official portal of the government — cybercrime.gov.in — to file complaints for cyber fraud, hacking, phishing or other digital offences. The portal accepts complaints from across India.

( ℹ ) Keep screenshots and transaction IDs ready before submitting — it helps track the case faster.

National Helpline Number

For immediate assistance or to report financial cyber-fraud, dial the national cybercrime helpline 1930 (toll-free). This connects you to the official Indian Cyber Crime Coordination Centre (I4C) helpline.

( ℹ ) Call 1930 urgently if you suspect your bank or wallet account has been compromised.

Data Breach / Hacking Assistance

If your personal info (passwords, email, phone) is leaked or your account is hacked — report immediately on the portal or contact local cyber-crime cell. Prompt reporting increases chance of retrieving stolen data.

( ℹ ) Change all passwords, enable two-factor authentication, and save any suspicious messages/screenshots before filing complaint.

Banking / Wallet Fraud Recovery

For financial fraud (UPI scams, fake transactions, wallet hacks) — call 1930 immediately. Authorities coordinate with banks and payment platforms to freeze suspicious transactions and attempt fund recovery.

( ℹ ) Don’t wait — report within first few hours. Keep transaction details, receipts, screenshots ready.

Legal Complaint / FIR Process

If online abuse, threats, fraud or identity theft happens — you can file a legal complaint at your local cyber-police station or lodge complaint via the portal. For major fraud (large amounts), many state police now accept e-FIRs for cybercrime complaints.

( ℹ ) Collect all proof (screenshots, chats, transaction IDs) and save acknowledgment from portal/helpline — it helps when filing FIR.

Local / State Cyber-Cell Contacts

Many states maintain dedicated cyber-crime cells under police departments. For example, if you are in Uttar Pradesh — contact the cyber cell listed at your district police HQ. For <state-specific details>, search “[Your State] cyber cell contact number” on Google or visit state police website.

( ℹ ) Always note the date, time, complaint number when contacting local police or cyber cell for tracking later.
